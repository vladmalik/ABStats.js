Balance sensitivity and false positive risk

Since you don't know if there is an effect or not, make sure you have high sensitivity to detect a true effect if it's there but a low risk of detecting a similar effect just by chance.

False positive rate is 1 in 20

This report sets alpha at 0.05. This means that if you call any result that reaches a p-value of 0.05, then your false positive rate will be 1 in 20 or less. This will be true no matter how long you run your test, though the effect size detected will decrease if you run tests longer. If you regularly peek and decide to keep going as your tests run, then your false positive rate will increase.
